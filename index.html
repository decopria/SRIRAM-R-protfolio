Projects

  • Arduino based Obstacle Avoiding Car

    Demonstrated an autonomous vehicle using ultrasonic sensors and Arduino microcontrollers. Presented in the SRM IST Tiruchirappalli Annual Project Expo conducted in June 2022, this project aimed to demonstrate an autonomous vehicle capable of navigating its environment without human intervention. The car was equipped with ultrasonic sensors that constantly scanned the surroundings, detecting obstacles in its path. Upon detecting an obstacle, the Arduino microcontroller processed the sensor data and instructed the car to change its direction, thereby avoiding collisions. Through meticulous design and programming, the project team successfully implemented algorithms for obstacle detection and avoidance, showcasing the potential of Arduino-based systems in real-world applications.

  • RFID Scanner using IoT

    Aimed to streamline attendance tracking through RFID technology and IoT connectivity. Each participant received a unique RFID tag for identification. As individuals entered designated areas like classrooms or event venues, the RFID scanner automatically detected their presence by reading these tags. Data collected by the scanner was transmitted in real-time to a central database via IoT, updating attendance records seamlessly. This integration reduced administrative workload and potential errors. Additionally, the system offered analytics and reporting features for insights into attendance trends. Overall, the project showcased how technology can efficiently manage attendance, benefiting education and beyond.
